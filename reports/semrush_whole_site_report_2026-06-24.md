## Executive Read

ConcreteWaco.com is still visible for the most important local contractor terms, but the ranking footprint is thin and over-dependent on a small set of commercial keywords. Semrush shows organic keywords down while traffic is slightly up, which usually means the site is holding a few valuable terms but losing breadth.

The highest-value work is content quality and topical completeness, not visual redesign. The site needs more practical, locally grounded answers for buyers who are comparing contractors, planning permits, evaluating foundation/slab issues, and trying to understand whether repair, replacement, drainage, demolition, or retaining walls are the right next step.

The biggest off-site weakness is trust. Authority Score is low, most referring domains are very low-authority, anchor text is mostly generic or URL-only, and the backlink network is flagged as suspicious. The right response is not more random directory links. It is citation cleanup, canonical consistency, topical local links, GBP/review activity, and proof-backed local trust assets.

## Content Quality Priorities

### Priority 1 - Add a slab permit guide

Recommended URL:

- `/guides/do-i-need-a-permit-to-pour-a-concrete-slab-waco-tx`

Target queries:

- `do i need a permit to pour a concrete slab`
- `do you need a permit to pour a concrete slab`
- `concrete slab permit waco tx`
- `driveway apron permit waco`

Content angle:

- Explain when a small slab usually does or does not need extra review.
- Cover right-of-way, driveway approaches, sidewalks, drainage, commercial properties, and HOA/property constraints.
- Make it clear this is not legal advice and that requirements depend on city/county scope.
- Link to driveway, patio, slab, repair, and Waco contractor pages.

Why it matters:

- Competitors are already visible for these low-difficulty questions.
- This builds practical trust with buyers before they request a bid.
- It supports service pages with internal links and FAQ schema.

### Priority 2 - Strengthen Lorena hardscaping relevance

Semrush shows `hardscaping contractor lorena` ranking with the Hewitt page at position 49. That is a relevance mismatch.

Recommended actions:

- Add Lorena-specific hardscaping copy to `src/data/locationPages.js`.
- Include patios, walks, retaining walls, driveway tie-ins, drainage transitions, and outdoor concrete layouts.
- Add a natural internal link from `/hardscaping-waco-tx` to the Lorena page.
- Add a Lorena FAQ about hardscaping and drainage.

### Priority 3 - Push retaining walls into top 3

The site is close for `retaining wall installation mclennan county` and `retaining wall installation woodway`.

Recommended actions:

- Keep the retaining wall service page focused on drainage, clay pressure, slope control, patio edges, and driveway transitions.
- Add stronger internal links from Woodway, hardscaping, patios, and drainage/repair content.
- Add a Waco-specific FAQ using `retaining wall installation in Waco` naturally.

### Priority 4 - Build foundation repair authority

The foundation repair page has visibility but no traffic.

Recommended actions:

- Keep language practical and honest: inspection, cracks, drainage, slab movement, repair vs replacement, and when a specialist/engineer may be needed.
- Add project photos or owner-approved examples if available.
- Internally link from concrete repair, driveway repair, demolition, patios, and location pages.
- Add FAQ schema around `foundation repair waco`, `foundation repair waco tx`, and `concrete slab foundation repair`.

### Priority 5 - Capture `waco cement` without sounding unnatural

People search "cement" when they mean concrete. The site ranks position 19 for `waco cement`, while competitors are stronger.

Recommended actions:

- Add a helpful FAQ: "Is cement the same as concrete?"
- Explain that cement is an ingredient in concrete.
- Use it to guide users toward concrete driveways, patios, slabs, and repair services.
- Do not stuff "cement contractor" across the site. One or two natural explanations are enough.

### Priority 6 - Add demolition/foundation removal content if accurate

Competitor visibility suggests demand for:

- `concrete foundation removal`
- `foundation demolition`

Recommended action:

- If SLA Concrete Works actually offers this scope, add a section and FAQ to the demolition page.
- If not, do not chase the keyword. Content should match real services.

## Off-Site and Local Trust Priorities

### Citation cleanup

Standardize listings and backlinks where possible to:

```text
Concrete Works LLC
https://www.concretewaco.com/
(254) 230-3102
Waco, TX service area
```

Focus first on profiles that can influence local search and AI/local citations:

1. Google Business Profile
2. Yelp
3. Bing Places
4. Apple Maps
5. Chamber/local business directories
6. BuildZoom, Porch, Angi, Thumbtack, HomeGuide if owner/account access allows

### Link quality

Target fewer, better links:

- Local suppliers or partners.
- Chamber or local business associations.
- Community sponsorship pages.
- Project or case-study mentions with real photos.
- Local articles, vendor pages, and neighborhood/community resources.

Avoid:

- Automated directory blasts.
- Low-quality SEO network pages.
- Irrelevant `.info` or foreign-language directory spam.
- Exact-match anchor manipulation.
